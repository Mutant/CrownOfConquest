About Kingdoms

Gameplay

Here's a brief explanation of what the game is - but the best way to find out what it's about is to dive right in... Click here to register.

(Note, as Kingdoms is currently in pre-beta, a few of the features below haven't been released yet - but expect to see them over the coming months).

Kingdoms is a "persistent world" role-playing game, where you control a party of characters, exploring a dynamic world, battling creatures, completing quests, discovering dungeons, and interacting with other parties.

Your party is made up of characters from 4 different classes - Warrior, Archer, Priest and Mage. As with most role-playing games, they're fairly puny to begin with, but after a few battles, and a few completed quests, they will increase in level, and can afford a some better weapons and armour. After a while, they'll even be strong enough to find and explore some of the dungeons scattered about the map, and they may even be able to afford to recruit new members to their ranks.

As your party gains in stature, they'll begin to have a greater impact on the world around them, helping to improve (or hinder!) the prosperity of towns, create landmarks, become involved in politics and many other actions you don't expect to find in a traditional RPG.

Your actions within the game are limited by turns, which are refreshed each day. If you don't use your turns on a particular day, they'll accumulate for a couple of days, meaning you don't have to log in every day.

Pre-beta

What's a pre-beta? Isn't that just an alpha? (I hear you ask).

Well, maybe.... when a game goes into "beta", it usually means it's pretty much finished, and all that's left is some final tweaking and bug fixing. In the case of Kingdoms, the game is far from finished. But because it's web-based, and in the interests of releasing early and often (which hopefully makes for a better game), Kingdoms has taken more of a "Google" approach to a beta. And since "beta" has a different meaning for games, we decided to call it a "pre-beta". The idea is to get as much feedback as possible from players, improving as we go.

During the pre-beta, you can usually expect to see at least one release a month, but sometimes as many as one a week. Each release usually contains one major new feature, plus several minor ones (and some bug fixes of course). So you can expect to see the game changing and improving constantly, and have a chance to contribute feedback, or even make suggestions for features to be included. For more on what you can expect to see, check out the Roadmap.

So when will it be finished? It's hard to say exactly, and maybe it'll never be completely finished. But as far as the major "core" of the game, we'd estimate that it's roughly 40% done. So there's a lot more to come!

Roadmap

Here are a few of the things you can expect to see added to Kingdoms in the coming months. In the interestes of not giving everything away, only a sample of features planned are listed here. Despite what you might think, there is a "grand vision" of what the game is going to be when finished. Some features might seem a little bit out of place, but everything is actually a piece in the puzzle, and will fit in once the other bits click into place.

A few upcoming features (in no particular order):

  • Magical Items - a bit different to upgraded items, as they'll be more than just stat increases
  • Leaderboard Enhancments - see where your party (and each individual character) ranks
  • More spells - including non-combat spells
  • Town raids - loot towns while the guards are (hopefully) sleeping
  • Dungeon enhancments - including traps, secret doors, treaure chests, multiple levels and more
  • Trade - between members of your guild, or anyone in the world - for the right price
